Vitruvian Man

The Vitruvian Man is a drawing rather than a painting, and it stands as one of Leonardo da Vinci's most recognized and influential works. This drawing illustrates ideal human proportions based on the writings of the Roman architect Vitruvius. The Vitruvian Man showcases Leonardo's deep fascination with the relationship between art, science, and the human body. The drawing is celebrated for its meticulous detail and its embodiment of the Renaissance ideal of blending artistic beauty with scientific precision, demonstrating the harmonious proportions of the human form.
